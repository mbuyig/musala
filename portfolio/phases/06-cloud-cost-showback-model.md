# Phase 6 - Cloud Cost Showback Model

**Status:** Not started  
**Roadmap phase:** May 2027  
**Related certification:** FinOps Certified Practitioner  
**Related commercial offer:** Cloud Cost Optimisation Sprint  

## Alignment

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Business problem | Acme can see AWS spend but cannot consistently attribute it to teams, products and environments or turn it into action. |
| Target audience | Engineering director, platform lead, FinOps lead, finance partner and product owners |
| Previous foundation | Tagging, account and EKS allocation concepts from earlier phases |
| Enables next | FinOps-aware enterprise architecture and executive governance reporting |
| Consulting angle | Show how cost visibility becomes an operating process, not merely a dashboard. |

## Scope

### In Scope

- Tagging policy
- Cost-allocation model
- AWS Budgets and alerting concept
- Showback dashboard
- Cost and Usage Report design
- Athena and QuickSight reporting concept
- Sample executive cost report
- Rightsizing, Savings Plans and RI evaluation process
- Waste-detection process and optimisation backlog
- EKS cost-allocation section
- AI or data workload cost-governance notes where relevant

### Out Of Scope

- Real production billing data
- Full chargeback implementation
- Procurement negotiation
- Automated remediation of every cost issue

## Evidence Layers

### Executive Layer

- Sample monthly cloud-cost report
- Top optimisation recommendations with estimated impact
- Explanation of showback versus chargeback

### Architecture Layer

- Cost-data flow diagram
- Tagging and allocation model
- ADR: showback-first approach
- Notes on EKS and shared-service allocation trade-offs

### Engineering Layer

- Synthetic sample data or dashboard mock-up
- Budget-alert configuration example
- Optimisation-backlog template
- Rightsizing and commitment-discount review checklist

## Ten-Minute Review Path

1. Read the executive cost report.
2. Inspect the allocation model and cost-data flow.
3. Review the tagging policy and showback dashboard.
4. Walk through the optimisation backlog.
5. Review the EKS allocation and commitment-discount notes.

## Definition Of Done

- [ ] The tagging and cost-allocation policy is documented.
- [ ] A showback dashboard or realistic mock-up exists.
- [ ] The executive cost report is complete.
- [ ] Rightsizing, waste and Savings Plans/RI processes are documented.
- [ ] EKS allocation is explained.
- [ ] A one-page case study and 90-second talk track are complete.
